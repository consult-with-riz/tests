SECTION 01 WHAT A STRONG LINKEDIN ABOUT SECTION NEEDS TO DO
# Criterion What it means in practice
01 Opens in the reader's world, not the writer's The first visible line, before the 'see more' fold, names the reader's situation, not the author's background. A buyer or an investor has 10 seconds. If the opener is about you, they are already gone.
02 Delivers proof before paragraph three The most credible facts about the business should appear within the first 3-4 lines. Proof buried deep in the section is proof most visitors never see.
03 Speaks to one specific person The About section should feel like it was written for the exact reader who most needs to act. When it tries to speak to everyone, it connects with no one.
04 Makes the next step obvious A reader who reaches the end and thinks 'this is relevant' needs somewhere to go. A specific CTA with a link, not a vague invitation, is what converts a profile visit into an inquiry.
05 Sounds like a person, not a company Founders have a voice. The About section should carry it. Passive constructions, third-person formality, and corporate framing all create distance from the reader that the founder's actual personality would not.

SECTION 04 WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

Each criterion from Section 01, mapped to the specific decision made in the rewrite.

Criterion Before After
Opens in the reader's world, not the writer's The current version opens well with "You're a founder, a hiring manager, or an operator, " placing the reader's frustration first, but immediately splits attention across three different personas. The rewrite opens with "Your team is losing hours every week to work that should run itself, " a single, concrete pain statement that any ops-burdened team lead recognizes instantly without needing to self-select from a list.
Delivers proof before paragraph three The strongest proof point, the Upwork proposal time dropping from 20-25 minutes to 3 minutes, is buried in paragraph five behind education, KPMG, Carbonteq, and Career Launchpad context. That same proof point now lands in paragraph three, immediately after the hook and the one-line introduction, so the reader sees measurable impact before they ever scroll.
Speaks to one specific person The opener addresses "a founder, a hiring manager, or an operator, " which is three separate ICPs with different motivations, making the section feel like it is hedging rather than targeting. The rewrite speaks to one person throughout: the team lead or founder who is watching manual processes drain capacity. Every line, from the opening pain to the closing CTA, is written for that single reader.
Makes the next step obvious The CTA includes a specific email and "connect here, " which gives the reader two options, but the email sits as plain text with no context for what happens after they reach out. The rewrite closes with "Reply to my message or hit Connect. I respond to every one, " which removes the exposed email (better handled in the message itself) and makes the action immediate and frictionless within LinkedIn.
Sounds like a person, not a company The current version already sounds like Ahmad. Short sentences, first person, direct tone. Lines like "That's what I do" and "Let's talk" carry real personality. The rewrite preserves that voice entirely. Short declarative lines. No passive constructions. No corporate hedging. The only change is tightening structure so the personality hits faster and does not get diluted by a long credential walkthrough in the middle.
